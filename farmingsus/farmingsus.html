Why farm sustainably?

how we farm regeneratively

Cover Crops

Instead of leaving bare soil for months after harvest, we choose to plant cover crops in our fields. These can be a variety of crops but we like to choose cover crops that both help to regenerate our soil and those that benefit the local ecology, such as birds and small mammals. By planting cover crops and farming more naturally, we reduce our reliance on artificial inputs by 10% on average each year, and do our bit for climate change by sequestering carbon dioxide all year round.

Minimum Tillage

Traditional agriculture previously relied on ploughing to prepare the seed bed for the next crop, heavily disturbing the soil. However, we choose not to plough where we can to reduce soil disturbance to a minimum. Minimum soil disturbance means more goodness (carbon dioxide and various fertilisers) is kept in the soil for the next crop. Additionally, by not disturbing the soil structure, we improve soil drainage and microbiology richness which helps to protect the local environment from extreme weather.

Livestock Integration

Because we are a mixed farm, we aim to integrate our livestock and arable enterprises as much as possible. One example is by growing cover crops on our arable fields that the sheep flock can graze on. This is both great for the health of our flock but also the health of the soil as natural processes are taking place. Another example is more common - we spread our cattle's manure on our fields because it is full of fertiliser for the crops, further reducing our reliance on artificial fertilisers.
