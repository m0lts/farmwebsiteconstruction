Lamb

A year for the sheep typically begins in October on Drivers Farms when a couple of rams are put into the field with the ewes, hoping to begin lambing at the start of March. During the winter period, the ewes are moved to our arable fields that have been planted with nutritious feed for them, such as stubble turnips, so when it comes around to lambing time in March, the ewes are in great shape to raise their lambs. Throughout the lambing period, the ewes are housed indoor at night, and outside in daytime, so that we can make sure that we are able to help the ewes whilst lambing. Once lambed, the ewe and her lambs stay indoors for a couple of weeks until the lambs are big enough to go out into the fields. This ensures that our lambs are able to cope with whatever weather is thrown their way. The lambs then stay out in the fields for the rest of their life feeding on grassland pasture only.
